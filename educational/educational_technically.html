Is Harry Potter even that good of a wizard?

The 'Chosen' One

(So they say)

Harry Potter is a young wizard born in Godricks Hollow, where his parents were murdered by He Who Must Not Be Named. Raised by his muggle aunt and uncle, he lived a life largely unaware of Wizard kind until he attended Hogwarts.
Once in school, Harry did a miriad of things:

  • Dispatching trolls
  • Killing a snake
  • Aiding and abetting an escaped criminal
  • Illegally entering and competing in the tri-wizard tournament
  • Starting a gang under the guize of "Dumbledore's Army"
  • Allowing He Who Must Not Be Named to return
  • Skipping a whole year to find some guy
One thing that is interesting, is that Harry Potter was never alone. He had the help of a Ronald Weasley, beefcake, and son of Authur Weasley, a ministry employee, and one Hermione Granger, muggle born, and top of her the class in almost every metric.

Harry Potter would not have been able to do any of the things he is praised for without their help. In fact, he most likely wouldn't have been able to do ANY of the things he did without them. Between Hermione's curious mind and Ron's intimate knowledge of the Wizarding World, they led and, honestly, mentored Harry Potter through most, if not all of his endeavors, including his crimes.
